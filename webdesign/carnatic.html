Carnatic Classical Music

Carnatic music is a classical music tradition from South India, known for its rich melodic and rhythmic complexity. It is one of the two major classical music systems in India (the other is Hindustani music of the North).

Key features of Carnatic music

1. Raga (Melody Framework)
  • A raga is a set of notes and rules that define how melodies are created
  • Each raga has its own mood, color, and emotional expression.
  • Carnatic music uses a highly structured system of ragas, including the 72 Melakarta ragas (parentscales).
2. Tala (Rhythm Cycle)
  • Tala refers to a rhythmic cycle, often intricate and mathematically precise.
  • Common talas include Adi tala, Rupaka tala, and Khanda Chapu.
  • Performers improvise within these rhythmic frameworks.

Themes

Most lyrics are devotional, often dedicated to Hindu deities, but also include philosophical and expressive themes.

Compositions

Carnatic music has a vast repertoire of compositions, typically devotional in nature. Major forms include:

  • Kriti / Keerthana (the most common, structured song form)
  • Varnam(foundational practice piece)
  • Alapana, Neraval, Kalpana Swara(improvisational elements)
  • Tillana, Padam, Javalis(different lyrical and rhythmic styles)

Improvisation

Improvisation is central to performance, but it follows strict rules of the raga and tala. Popular improvisational forms:

  • Raga Alapana-melodic exploration without rhythm
  • Tanam- rhythmic improvisation
  • Pallavi (as part of Ragam, Tanam, Pallavi)-a creative centerpiece of concerts
